Exhibit 1 — the address space

Where the heap lands vs. where the engine expects it

School of Chaos Classic's engine assumes its heap never needs an address above 16GB. On a 64-bit process, Android's ASLR is free to place that heap anywhere in a much larger range — including above that line.

When the placement lands past 16GB, the allocator's packed pointer representation overflows. What actually gets dereferenced next is a corrupted, near-zero address — observed directly in crash logs:

Background

The game

School of Chaos Classic (com.vnlentertainment.socclassic) is a school-life sim/RPG by VNL Entertainment — schoolyard exploration, quests, a Market Place, character progression. It's built on Unity 2017.4.34f1 and ships 64-bit only. Both facts turn out to matter directly.

Symptom

What happens

The game gets through the splash screen and into the loading scene, then dies silently back to the home screen — no dialog, no ANR. First identified on a Pixel 9 Pro XL and initially assumed to be a high-RAM-Pixel issue. It is not — see confirmed devices below.

The crash is also intermittent on any one device, which is a direct consequence of the cause, not a flaky install.

Root cause

Why it happens

Pulling the APK and inspecting libunity.so confirms Unity 2017.4.34f1 — years before Unity properly supported large 64-bit address spaces. The crash string sits directly next to the engine's own allocator diagnostics inside the binary: 

Could not allocate memory: System out of memory!
Trying to allocate: %zuB with %zu alignment. MemoryLabel: %s
Allocation happend at: Line:%d in %s
[ %s ] used: %zuB | peak: %zuB | reserved: %zuB
Using memoryadresses from more that 16GB of memory

That allocator packs its bookkeeping assuming addresses stay under ~16GB (2³⁴). When ASLR places the heap above that line, the packed pointer overflows, corrupts memory, and the app segfaults — almost always right as the first asset bundle loads.

Revised understanding
This was first framed as a "16GB+ RAM phones" issue, since it showed up reliably on a 16GB Pixel. Having now seen the identical crash on manufacturers where the RAM tier isn't confirmed to be 16GB, the more accurate framing is Android's ASLR entropy in general — which has trended upward across OS versions — rather than a strict RAM cutoff.
